# Feature Requirements

## User Accounts And Profiles

Profiles should include name, profile photo, current books, completed books, favorite genres, reading streak, Reading Momentum score, achievements, recent reviews, reading history, and discussion history.

Reason: Profiles create ownership and make progress visible.

## Private Reading Groups

Groups should include members, a reading feed, weekly check-ins, discussion posts, book nominations, optional Book of the Month voting, group leaderboard, and group reading goals.

Reason: Small groups create social accountability.

## Current Book Tracking

Users should add one or more current books with title, author, format, genre, total pages or chapters when available, current page or chapter, reading goal type, start date, and optional target finish date.

Reason: Progress tracking makes reading visible and enables better prompts and fairer scoring.

## Reading Check-Ins

Users should be able to log pages read, chapters completed, minutes read, audiobook minutes listened, reading session completed, or skipped today.

Requirement: A check-in should take less than 30 seconds.

## Weekly Discussion Rhythm

Suggested rhythm:

- Monday: progress check-in and light reflection
- Thursday: discussion prompt and group conversation
- Weekend: recap, review prompts, and optional Book of the Month voting

Reason: Predictable rhythm creates structure without making the app feel demanding.

## AI Discussion Questions

The app should generate personalized questions based on the user's book, progress, and reading format.

Question types may include character reflection, theme analysis, prediction, personal connection, argument or critique, favorite moment, confusing moment, and emotional reaction.

Requirement: AI should prompt thinking, not replace reading.

## Group Feed

The feed should show meaningful activity such as check-ins, prompt answers, completed books, reviews, nominations, and streak milestones.

Requirement: The feed should avoid overloading users with every small action.

## Comments And Replies

The app should support short replies, reactions, encouragement, spoiler-safe comments, and threaded discussion.

## Review System

The review system should include live reviews while reading and final reviews after completion.

Live review examples:

- Chapter reaction
- Mid-book prediction
- Favorite quote
- Confusing section
- Character opinion

Final review fields:

- Overall rating
- Optional category ratings
- Short review
- Long review
- Recommended audience
- Favorite moment
- Pacing rating
- Writing rating
- Character rating
- Emotional impact rating
- Re-read potential

## Spoiler-Aware Review Timeline

Spoiler content should be hidden, blurred, locked until progress is reached, or clearly labeled.

Requirement: Users should not automatically see thoughts from later progress points than they have reached.

## Book Of The Month

Groups may optionally nominate and vote on a shared Book of the Month. No user should be required to read the winner.

Future weighting factors may include nominations, votes, genre fit, time since last nomination, past group interest, positive reviews, and randomness.
