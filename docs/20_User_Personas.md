# User Personas

Status: Draft
Version: 0.1
Owner: Product Manager

## Persona 1: The Busy Student

Profile:

- College student with irregular schedule
- Wants to read for fun but loses momentum during exams and deadlines
- Reads print, ebooks, and assigned material

Goal:

- Read consistently without feeling like reading is another assignment.

Pain points:

- Starts books and drops them.
- Feels behind quickly.
- Has limited time and attention.
- May read late at night or between classes.

MVP needs:

- Fast check-ins
- Grace days
- Supportive streak copy
- Mobile-first dashboard
- Flexible reading units

Trust concerns:

- Does not want friends judging slow progress.
- Needs private check-in options.

## Persona 2: The Casual Friend Group Reader

Profile:

- Reads occasionally
- Wants reading to feel social
- Dislikes formal book clubs

Goal:

- Stay connected with friends through reading without needing everyone to read the same book.

Pain points:

- Book clubs feel too rigid.
- Different friends read at different speeds.
- Group chats lose book discussion quickly.

MVP needs:

- Private groups
- Group feed
- Reactions and replies
- Different books in same group
- Optional shared book features later

Trust concerns:

- Wants low pressure and no public ranking by pages.

## Persona 3: The Young Professional Rebuilder

Profile:

- Used to read more
- Wants to reduce passive screen time
- Has job and life routines competing with reading

Goal:

- Rebuild a sustainable reading habit.

Pain points:

- Reads in bursts, then stops.
- Feels friction restarting after missed days.
- Wants progress to feel visible.

MVP needs:

- Comeback recognition
- Weekly rhythm
- Dashboard progress
- Reading history
- Gentle notifications

Trust concerns:

- Does not want guilt-based reminders.

## Persona 4: The Audiobook Listener

Profile:

- Reads mostly through audiobooks while commuting, walking, or doing chores
- May not know page numbers

Goal:

- Have audiobook listening treated as real reading.

Pain points:

- Page-based systems feel unfair.
- Spoiler gates may not map cleanly to timestamps.
- Completion and progress are time-based.

MVP needs:

- Audiobook minutes
- Time-based check-ins
- Completion support
- Format fairness
- Progress notes when page data is unavailable

Trust concerns:

- Does not want scoring to treat audiobooks as lesser.

## Persona 5: The Spoiler-Sensitive Reader

Profile:

- Enjoys discussion but strongly avoids spoilers
- May read slower than friends

Goal:

- Participate in social reading without accidentally seeing future content.

Pain points:

- Group discussion can reveal too much.
- Reviews often spoil endings.
- AI prompts can hint at future events.

MVP needs:

- Locked spoiler content
- Progress-based reveal
- Explicit spoiler labels
- Conservative AI prompts
- Safe defaults when progress is unknown

Trust concerns:

- One spoiler mistake can reduce trust in the whole app.

## Persona 6: The Deep Reflective Reader

Profile:

- Reads slowly and thoughtfully
- Likes notes, quotes, themes, and discussion
- May read nonfiction, literary fiction, academic texts, or philosophy

Goal:

- Capture thoughts while reading and discuss ideas meaningfully.

Pain points:

- Page-count systems undervalue slow reading.
- Final reviews miss how opinions changed over time.
- Wants depth without pressure.

MVP needs:

- Live review entries
- Reflection prompts
- Discussion posts
- Fair scoring based on reflection and consistency
- Optional long-form responses

Trust concerns:

- Does not want AI to flatten or replace their thinking.

## Persona-To-Feature Mapping

- Fast check-ins: Busy Student, Young Professional, Audiobook Listener
- Private groups: Casual Friend Group Reader, Spoiler-Sensitive Reader
- Spoiler locks: Spoiler-Sensitive Reader, Deep Reflective Reader
- Format fairness: Audiobook Listener, Deep Reflective Reader
- Comeback behavior: Busy Student, Young Professional
- Reflection prompts: Deep Reflective Reader, Casual Friend Group Reader
- Gentle notifications: Busy Student, Young Professional
