# Product Principles

## User Choice Comes First

Users should choose their own books, formats, goals, and pace.

Product decisions:

- Users can join the same group while reading different books.
- Users can optionally participate in a shared Book of the Month.
- Users can skip the group book without being punished.
- Users can set reading goals by pages, chapters, minutes, or sessions.

## Consistency Matters More Than Raw Volume

The product should reward repeated reading behavior more than one-time reading bursts.

Product decisions:

- The main score should prioritize weekly consistency.
- Long reads should earn bonus progress with diminishing returns.
- Streaks should include grace days.

## Reflection Improves The Reading Experience

Reading should not only be measured by pages completed. Prompts should help users notice themes, characters, ideas, emotions, predictions, and opinions.

Product decisions:

- Generate reflection prompts after check-ins.
- Make prompts spoiler-aware.
- Encourage original thought instead of summaries.
- Support both short and long answers.

## Accountability Should Feel Supportive

The app should avoid shame-based motivation. Leaderboards and points should support participation, not unhealthy comparison.

Product decisions:

- Avoid ranking users only by pages read.
- Highlight consistency, discussion, support, completion, and comeback progress.
- Recognize users for encouraging others.

## The App Should Increase Reading, Not Replace It

The goal is to increase real reading behavior, not maximize screen time.

Product decisions:

- Keep check-ins fast.
- Keep notifications useful and limited.
- Use weekly rituals instead of constant engagement loops.
- Make the dashboard guide users back to reading.
