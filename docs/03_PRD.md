# Product Requirements Document

Status: Draft
Version: 0.2
Owner: Product Manager

## Product Summary

Reading Momentum is a social reading accountability web app that helps people read more consistently with friends while allowing every user to read their own book, format, goal, and pace.

The product is built around one core belief: people often need structure, reflection, and supportive accountability more than they need another book tracker.

## Mission Statement

Help users keep reading when they otherwise would have stopped.

## Problem Statement

Many people want to read more, but struggle to maintain the habit. Reading competes with social media, streaming, work, school, and everyday responsibilities. Existing reading apps often emphasize tracking finished books, reviews, statistics, or discovery. Those are useful, but they do not fully solve the day-to-day momentum problem.

Reading Momentum focuses on the active habit loop:

- Choose what to read.
- Check in quickly.
- Reflect lightly.
- Share progress with trusted friends.
- Receive supportive accountability.
- Build a visible reading identity over time.

## Target Audience

Primary audience:

- College students
- Young professionals
- Friend groups
- Remote friend groups
- Casual readers
- People trying to reduce passive screen time
- People who enjoy books but dislike formal book clubs

Secondary future audiences:

- Schools
- Libraries
- Professional learning groups
- Corporate book clubs
- Creator-led reading communities
- Public reading circles

## MVP Objective

The MVP should prove whether small private groups help users return to reading consistently.

The MVP is successful if users:

- Add current books.
- Log reading check-ins repeatedly.
- Participate in light reflection.
- See meaningful group activity.
- Return after missing days.
- Feel supported rather than judged.

## MVP Scope

### Must Have

- Account creation and login
- Basic profile creation
- Private group creation and joining
- Current book tracking
- Reading check-ins
- Basic dashboard
- Group feed
- Discussion posts and replies
- AI-generated reflection prompts
- Basic streaks
- Basic XP event tracking
- Book completion
- Final review
- Simple multi-category leaderboard
- Spoiler-safe content rules

### Should Have

- Reactions
- Live review entries
- Book nominations
- Notification preferences
- Product analytics foundation
- Accessibility baseline

### Could Have

- Book of the Month voting
- Achievement displays
- Weekly recap
- Recommendation prompts
- Bookshelf customization

### Out Of MVP

- Native mobile app
- Public communities
- Advanced achievement system
- Full Book of the Month algorithm
- Kindle integration
- Goodreads import
- Reading Wrapped
- Advanced recommendation engine
- Advanced analytics dashboards
- Custom avatars

## Non-Goals

Reading Momentum should not:

- Replace Goodreads.
- Force every group member to read the same book.
- Make reading feel like homework.
- Optimize for time spent in app.
- Rank users primarily by pages read.
- Let AI replace reading, reflection, or human discussion.

## Core Product Principles

- User choice comes first.
- Consistency matters more than raw volume.
- Reflection improves the reading experience.
- Accountability should feel supportive.
- The app should increase reading, not replace it.

## Primary User Stories

### Reader

As a reader, I want to add my current book so I can make my reading visible.

As a reader, I want to check in quickly so I can maintain the habit without friction.

As a reader, I want to skip today without shame so I can return tomorrow.

As a reader, I want prompts that help me think without spoiling future content.

### Group Member

As a group member, I want to see what my friends are reading so the group feels alive.

As a group member, I want to reply and react supportively so friends feel seen.

As a group member, I want spoiler protection so I can participate safely.

### Group Owner

As a group owner, I want to create a private group so I can invite trusted friends.

As a group owner, I want basic membership control so the group stays comfortable.

## Success Metrics

MVP success should be measured by reading behavior, not app consumption.

Primary metrics:

- Weekly active users
- Weekly reading check-ins per user
- Week 4 retention
- Group retention
- Discussion participation rate
- Book completion rate
- Review completion rate
- Percentage of users returning after missing a week

Secondary metrics:

- Average replies per discussion post
- Prompt answer rate
- Private versus shared check-in ratio
- Notification opt-out rate
- User-reported pressure or comfort

Avoid optimizing for:

- Time spent in app
- Number of screens viewed
- Notification opens alone

## Key Risks

- Spoiler protection may be too ambiguous.
- Progress normalization across pages, chapters, minutes, and sessions may feel unfair.
- Check-in flow may become too crowded.
- Social sharing defaults may violate user expectations.
- Reflection scoring may feel judgmental.
- Notifications may feel like pressure.
